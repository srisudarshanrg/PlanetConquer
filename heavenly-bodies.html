The Solar System

The Solar System is a organization of our sun and 8 other planets. The 8 planets - Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune revolve around the sun in elliptical orbits. We will look into the sun and each of these planets in detail.

Mercury

It is the planet that is closest to the sun. Now you might be thinking... Being the closest to the sun it might also be the hottest planet! But that is not the case. Mercury has smouldering days, but it's nights are freezing cold as it has no atmosphere to save some of the heat captured in the day.
As Mercury is the closest to the sun, it has the smallest orbit, which makes it the planet that revolves the fastest.
Revolution1 time: 88 Earth Days
Rotation2 time: 59 Earth Days

Venus

It is the second planet in the solar system and is named after the Roman goddess of beauty because of how beautiful it looks when seen from Earth. It is the hottest planet as it has a dense atmosphere mainly composed of carbon dioxide, and this traps the sunlight it recieves during the day.
It is also known as the evening star as it appears very bright when seen from Earth in the evenings.
Revolution time: 225 Earth Days
Rotation time: 243 Earth Days

Earth

Earth! What to we not know about earth! It is the third planet from the sun and THE ONLY PLANET THAT SUPPORTS LIFE! 70% of Earth is covered with water while the rest 30% is covered by land. Earth has all the features needed for sustaining life! Ample amount of sunlight, plenty of water, surplus of resources! We have everything we need!
Revolution time: 365/366 days
Rotation time: 24 hours

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun. It is red in color but white at the poles. It's the closest planet to have life on it as frozen water has been found here. It is completely crater-pocked.
Revolution time: 365 days
Rotation time: 24 Hours 40 Minutes
